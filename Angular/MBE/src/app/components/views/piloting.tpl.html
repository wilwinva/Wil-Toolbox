Piloting MBE for the National Security Complex: MAP-R Project

The Product Lifecycle Management team is involved in several efforts to promote MBE within the nuclear security enterprise.

MAP-R

In collaboration with Kansas City Nuclear Security Campus (KS/NSC) and NNSA, the Product Lifecycle Management team is undertaking MAP-R, a project to quantify the business value for model-based definition, manufacturing, and inspection.

MAP-R will build on a study conducted by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) to help answer an array of questions:

  • Does MBE provide a quantifiable business benefit?
  • What are the attributes of a “trusted” model?
  • How does the national security enterprise authorize a model as formal product definition?
  • What are the current capabilities (people, processes, tools) and maturity related to the use of models in NW development?

JTD Engineering Design Activity

Sandia will support the design engineering portion of a Joint Technology Demonstrator initiative to pilot an MBE process for development and deployment. This initiative includes participation of the three NNSA laboratories, KC/NSC, NNSA, US Air Force, US Navy, and several United Kingdom organizations: the Royal Navy, Ministry of Defense and Atomic Weapons Establishment (AWE).

The project will examine:

  • A CAD-driven product structure
  • Integrated change management
  • Improved model quality rules
  • Enhanced collaboration with need-to-know enforced and published 3D viewables
  • Data authorization via technical data packages

MBx Summit 2017

We are partnering with multiple Sandia organizations to coordinate an MBx Summit in 2017. This summit is seen as a venue for sharing information on the focus and progress of MBE initiatives throughout Sandia and potentially for integrating efforts with common features and goals.
